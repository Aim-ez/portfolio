Overview

PlotLine is a social reading application designed to make discovering and sharing books feel more engaging. Inspired by the idea of "Goodreads, but more fun," it combines reading lists, reviews, and social features with a colourful, modern interface designed to make spending time in the app enjoyable.

Why I Built It

As someone who loves reading, I often found reading apps functional but uninspiring. They tended to prioritize information over experience, with interfaces that felt dated and lacked personality. When my project parter and I were brainstorming ideas for our semester project, we realized we both wanted something that made reading feel a little more vibrant. PlotLine becamse our take on a social reading platform that focused just as much on the experience as the features.

My Role

My primary focus was designing and developing the application's user experience. I created the overal visual identity, designed the interface, devloped the frontend in React Native, and created our backend and database integration. I also designed the PlotLine name and logo, helping to establish the application's personality from the ground up.

Challenges

One of the biggest challenges was implementing user authentication. It was the first time I had built a system where users could securely create accounts, log in, and maintain personalized profiles. Understanding how the frontend, backend, database, and authentication logic all worked together pushed me well beyond anything I'd built before.

What I'm Most Proud Of

What I'm most proud of isn't a single feature. It's how much we accomplished in one semester. Neither of us had built a full-stack application before or worked with React Native, yet within three and a half months we designed, built, tested, and polished a complete application from the ground up. Looking back, I'm not only proud of the finished product, but of how quickly we learned unfamiliar technologies and turned an idea into something people could actually use.

Design Philosophy

From the beginning, I wanted PlotLine to feel welcoming and colourful. Reading is something I genuinely enjoy, and I wanted the interface to reflect that enthusiasm rather than feeling like another productivity app. Designing the UI from scratch gave me the opportunity to think carefully about colour, typography, spacing, and consistency. It was an experience that sparked my interst in creating software that is not only functional but enjoyable to use.
